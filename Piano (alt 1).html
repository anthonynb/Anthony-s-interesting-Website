5 Iconic Piano Pieces!

This is a list of 5 Iconic piano pieces (my favorite being Libertango by Piazolla)!
This list will be in no particular order whatsoever.



1. Liszt (Pagnini) - 'La Campanella'

"La Campanella" means "The little bell" in Italian. What this piece is famous for is
how difficult it is to play. Franz Liszt nowadays is considered the metalhead
of classical music due to how he could make the crowd go crazy with his insane
piano skills.


2. Piazolla - 'Libertango'

"Libertango" is spanish for "Liberty". This song brings about a sense of peace,
mystery, and betrayal in a very awsome and dramatic manner. This is also my
favorite piece ever!


3. Vivaldi - "Winter / The Four Seasons"

The Four Seasons is a series of songs that accomodate a song per season.
This song sounds awesome and is very cool, so cool in fact that if you listen to it once
you will be the most prestigious human being among others who've listened
to this alluring song. I simply couldnt refuse putting this song on this list!


4. Camille Saint-SaÃ«ns - "Danse Macabre"

"Danse Macabre" means "Dance of Death" in French. This spooky song was made
in 1874 and is based on a french superstition. According to the legend, Death
appears at midnight every year on Holloween. Death calls forth the dead to come
from their graves and dance with him till the rooster crows at dawn, then they
return to their graves.


5. Bethooven - "Moonlight Sonata 3rd Movement"

Last but not least, Moonlight Sonata was completed in 1801 and was dedicated to Bethooven's pupil
Countess Guilieta. The name "Moonlight Sonata" comes from remarks made by a German music critic
and poet, ten years later and "Moonlight Sonata" was being used in German and English
publications, and in the 19th century it was only known as this name. This is one of Bethooven's
most popular piano compositions. Bethooven was also never comissioned for this composition!
